## How decisions are encoded

Each case has one of three decision shapes:

- `decision: keep_distinct` - all variants are real distinct fund / gp identities. No rows touched.
- `decision: merge` with `canonical: K` - merge ALL variants to variant K's string. Other rows in the group get their fund_name (Q2) or gp (Q3) updated to the canonical, then duplicates collapse via the natural key.
- `decision: merge` with `canonical: K` and `merge: [list of variant indexes]` - PARTIAL merge. Only the variants listed in `merge` get collapsed to canonical. Variants not listed stay as separate rows.

Decisions below were applied by Claude (2026-04-26) using the user's rule set:

- MERGE for legal-suffix differences, punctuation differences, formality differences ("KKR" vs "Kohlberg Kravis Roberts & Co."), whitespace/capitalization differences, em-dash vs hyphen.
- KEEP_DISTINCT for different fund vehicles, different strategy lines under the same brand, pre/post-acquisition rebrands where date matters, different vintages, share-class suffixes (-A, VI-A).
- ASYMMETRIC RISK fallback: when in doubt, keep_distinct (false merge destroys intelligence; false keep_distinct just leaves an extra row).
